# Pishacha

## Overview
**Pishachas** (also pisacha, pishach, piśāca) are flesh-eating demons in Hindu mythology that possess humans, cause madness and disease, and inhabit cremation grounds. They represent one category of malevolent beings in Hindu supernatural taxonomy and appear in ancient Sanskrit texts dating back thousands of years.

**Status**: Mythological beings  
**Category**: Hindu Mythology  
**Period**: Ancient to Present  
**Region**: Indian Subcontinent

## Description

### Physical Characteristics
Traditional descriptions of pishachas include:
- **Dark Complexion**: Often described as dark or black
- **Corpse-Like**: Resembling dead bodies
- **Red Eyes**: Glowing or red eyes
- **Ugly Appearance**: Repulsive, frightening features
- **Emaciated**: Thin, skeletal appearance
- **Protruding Veins**: Visible blood vessels
- **Varied Forms**: Can change appearance

### Abilities and Powers
Pishachas are believed to:
- **Possess Humans**: Enter and control human bodies
- **Cause Madness**: Drive victims insane
- **Spread Disease**: Inflict physical illness
- **Induce Degradation**: Make people perform shameful acts
- **Feed on Flesh**: Consume human corpses
- **Drink Blood**: Some accounts mention blood-drinking
- **Create Illusions**: Deceive and confuse humans
- **Speak Languages**: Communicate in "Paishachi" language

## Sanskrit and Textual Origins

### Ancient Sources
Pishachas appear in numerous Sanskrit texts:

#### Vedic Period
- **Atharva Veda**: Earliest references to pishachas
- **Rig Veda**: Mentions of malevolent spirits
- Described as nocturnal demons to be warded off

#### Puranas
- **Vishnu Purana**: Creation and nature of pishachas
- **Padma Purana**: Stories involving pishacha encounters
- **Garuda Purana**: Context in funeral and death rites
- Various Puranas place them in supernatural hierarchy

#### Other Texts
- **Mahabharata**: References in epic narratives
- **Ramayana**: Mentions in context of demons
- **Manusmriti**: Legal/social text mentioning impurity associations
- **Katha Sarit Sagara**: Tales involving pishachas

### Etymology
- **Sanskrit**: पिशाच (piśāca)
- **Meaning**: Possibly related to "flesh" (piśita)
- **Language**: Paishachi Prakrit named after these demons
- **Regional Names**: Various names in different Indian languages

## Mythology and Creation

### Origin Stories
Different texts provide various origins:

#### Brahma's Creation
- Created from Brahma's breath
- Emerged during creation along with other beings
- Part of divine plan for cosmic balance

#### Vice and Emotion
- Arose from negative qualities like pride, anger, envy
- Manifestation of human wickedness
- Punishment for sins in previous lives

#### Natural Order
- Part of the supernatural hierarchy
- Among lowest class of beings
- Necessary component of cosmic order

## Behavior and Habitat

### Preferred Locations
Pishachas inhabit:
- **Cremation Grounds**: Primary dwelling place
- **Cemeteries**: Among corpses
- **Dark Places**: Abandoned buildings, forests
- **Crossroads**: Liminal spaces
- **Unclean Areas**: Places of impurity
- **Battlefields**: Among the dead

### Activities
Traditional beliefs about pishacha behavior:
- Feeding on corpses and funeral offerings
- Possessing vulnerable individuals
- Causing nightmares and bad dreams
- Spreading fear and chaos
- Tempting humans toward sin
- Interfering with religious practices

### Victims and Targets
Pishachas particularly affect:
- Those in impure states
- People near cremation grounds
- Individuals who break religious rules
- Those with weak spiritual defenses
- Pregnant women and children (vulnerable)
- People engaged in sinful behavior

## Possession and Symptoms

### Signs of Possession
Traditional indicators include:
- **Mental Changes**: Madness, confusion, personality shifts
- **Physical Symptoms**: Disease, pain, weakness
- **Behavioral Changes**: Shameful or degrading acts
- **Speech Changes**: Different voice or language
- **Appearance Changes**: Altered facial features
- **Aversion to Sacred**: Rejection of religious items
- **Abnormal Strength**: Superhuman abilities

### Traditional Explanations
Possession explained various conditions:

- Neurological conditions
- Infectious diseases
- Behavioral problems
- Social deviance

## Protection and Exorcism

### Preventive Measures
Traditional protections include:
- **Mantras**: Sacred verses and prayers
- **Rituals**: Religious ceremonies and offerings
- **Purity**: Maintaining cleanliness and virtue
- **Amulets**: Protective talismans
- **Sacred Items**: Holy ash, water, symbols
- **Avoidance**: Staying away from cremation grounds at night
- **Proper Behavior**: Following dharmic principles

### Exorcism Methods
To remove pishacha possession:
- **Mantras**: Specific vedic verses
- **Brahmin Intervention**: Priests performing rituals
- **Offerings**: Appeasement through gifts
- **Fire Rituals**: Havans and sacred fires
- **Sacred Substances**: Holy water, ash, herbs

### Deities for Protection
Gods invoked against pishachas:
- **Shiva**: Dwells in cremation grounds, controls demons
- **Kali**: Powerful against malevolent spirits
- **Hanuman**: Protector against evil forces
- **Durga**: Destroyer of demons
- **Various Protective Deities**: Regional variations

## Hindu Supernatural Taxonomy

### Classification of Beings
In Hindu cosmology, pishachas ranked among:
- **Lower Beings**: Bottom of supernatural hierarchy
- **Asuras**: Demons (broader category)
- **Different from Rakshasas**: More powerful demon class
- **Similar to Pretas**: Hungry ghosts
- **Distinct from Bhutas**: General spirits

### Comparison with Other Demons

#### Rakshasas
- **Rakshasas**: More powerful, often shapeshifters
- **Pishachas**: Lower, primarily corpse-eaters and possessors

#### Pretas
- **Pretas**: Hungry ghosts of the dead
- **Pishachas**: Never human, demonic from creation

#### Bhutas
- **Bhutas**: General spirits and ghosts
- **Pishachas**: Specific demon category
